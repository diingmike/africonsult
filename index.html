East Africa - An Overview

The East African Community, an economic currently includes the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Somalia, Burundi, Kenya, Rwanda, South Sudan, Uganda and Tanzania. It has been the continentâ€™s fastest-growing region in recent years.

East Africa presents numerous opportunities for businesses across diverse sectors like agriculture, energy, technology, and tourism. The region boasts a large and increasingly mobile population, diverse natural resources, and a growing number of vibrant urban centers.

The EAC is investing in infrastructure projects, including transportation, energy, and communication, to enhance regional competitiveness.

East Africa has traditional resources including Mining; Fisheries; Tourism, and the recent addition of Vast Oil & Gas Reserves and Renewable Energy Solutions: Green Hydrogen development, allied with substantial Solar and Wind capacity. It has significant potential for renewable energy, particularly solar and wind power.

East Africa presents an excellent opportunity for investors, with a wide variety of profitable market sectors to explore.

The 2024 Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Report shows that East Africa increased by 35% to $8.2 billion.

East Africa is a significant producer of essential metals and minerals like gold, diamonds, uranium, platinum, nickel, bauxite, and cobalt, which significantly contribute to the region's GDP.

East Africa's manufacturing sector, notably in Kenya and Tanzania, is a vital industry producing a wide range of goods including textiles, leather, food, beverages, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, plastics, and metal products. Economic growth relies heavily on the energy sector, with investments in renewable energy sources like solar and wind power, and infrastructure development projects like the Standard Gauge Railway.

Active Tenders

The Kisumu International Convention Centre (KICC)

Kenya currently boasts only one purpose-built conference center, the Kenyatta International Conference Centre in Nairobi.

Recognizing the potential of Kisumu as a hub for business and tourism, the need for a second purpose-built convention center has emerged.

The Kisumu International Convention Centre (KICC) is proposed to attract conferences, revitalize the local economy, and position Kisumu as a year-round destination.

Kenya Off-Grid Solar Access Project (KOSAP)

Kenya's energy revolution will be significantly expanded with the establishment of 113 mini-grids in 14 counties through a Ksh.10 billion (USD $77.38 million) initiative. The beneficiary counties are Garissa, Isiolo, Kilifi, Kwale, Lamu, Mandera, Marsabit, Narok, Samburu, Taita Taveta, Tana River, Turkana, Wajir, and West Pokot.

The government is working with the African Development Bank and the World Bank to carry out the project.

The project will involve construction of 113 mini solar grids over the next 12 months. Additionally, diesel-powered stations are being hybridized with solar PV systems, with plans underway to install an extra 8MW of solar power in Wajir County.

50MW Kajiado Solar PV Project

The Kajiado Solar Plant aims to be largest grid connected solar power plant in East & Central Africa. Scope of works include the following;

  1. Construction of a 132KV bay at 132/33/11KV Ketraco Substation linking to the 132KV Bus bars.
  2. Construction of 5.7 km of 132KV transmission line with 16No. pylons (Towers) from the Solar Plant to Ketraco Substation.
  3. Establish a 33/132 KV substation at the Solar Plant with a 50 MVA 33/132KV step up transformer.
  4. Establish a 50 MVA (Installed capacity is 54.65MW) solar generation plant generation complete, with Solar panels, step up transformers -33no x 0.415/33kV transformers , indoor 33kV switch room and control room for monitoring, including CCTV installations for monitoring.

The project will involve implementation of a 50MW grid based solar power generation plant whereby all the generated power is to be sold to Kenya Power through a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA). To support the project, a 6 Kilometre 132 Kilo Vault (KV) power transmission line has been constructed between the REREC solar power plant and the Kenya Electricity Transmission Company (KETRACO) sub-station in Garissa to feed the power into the national power grid.

Affordable Housing

The Kenyan government's Affordable Housing Program (AHP) aims to provide decent and affordable housing to low and middle-income households.

Several private developers are involved in affordable housing in Nairobi and other parts of Kenya. Our client Mtwapa Investments Limited aims to put up A 220-unit affordable housing project in Mombasa County.
